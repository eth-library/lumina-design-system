# Visual Principles

Lumina visuals should make complex ideas understandable without making them feel simplistic.

They should communicate academic quality, technical confidence and human-centred intelligence.

Every illustration, diagram or visual concept should follow the principles below.

---

## 1. Knowledge First

Lumina is about knowledge.

Technology is important, but it is never the main character.

Visuals should focus on:

- documents
- metadata
- collections
- research outputs
- semantic relationships
- discovery
- understanding
- human expertise

Technical elements such as APIs, embeddings, agents and pipelines should appear as supporting structures that help knowledge become more accessible and reusable.

A good Lumina visual shows what technology enables, not just what technology is.

---

## 2. Human-Centred

People should remain visible in the Lumina visual identity.

They represent expertise, judgement, interpretation and responsibility.

People may appear as:

- librarians
- researchers
- students
- data specialists
- developers
- domain experts
- service owners

They should look focused, calm and professional.

Avoid visual narratives where technology acts independently from people.

Lumina supports human work; it does not replace human expertise.

---

## 3. Calm and Trustworthy

Lumina visuals should feel calm before they feel impressive.

Avoid visual drama.

Avoid unnecessary movement, glow, complexity or visual noise.

Use:

- whitespace
- balanced compositions
- restrained colour
- clear hierarchy
- moderate detail
- stable geometric forms

A calm visual language communicates maturity and trust.

This is especially important because Lumina works with knowledge, metadata, academic services and AI-supported workflows.

---

## 4. Clear Before Complex

Every visual should communicate one main idea.

The viewer should understand the purpose of the visual within a few seconds.

If a concept is complex, reduce it to its essential relationships.

Prefer:

- a small number of meaningful elements
- clear grouping
- simple flows
- readable structures
- recognisable visual metaphors

Avoid:

- dense networks
- overloaded dashboards
- too many icons
- unnecessary labels
- decorative complexity

Complex systems can be shown, but they must be visually organised.

---

## 5. Semantic, Not Decorative

Visual elements should have meaning.

Lines should represent relationships.

Cards should represent information objects.

Clusters should represent semantic proximity.

Layers should represent processing or enrichment.

Colours should support hierarchy or meaning.

Avoid decorative elements that do not contribute to the message.

Lumina visuals should feel intentional.

Nothing should appear random.

---

## 6. Structured and Modular

Lumina is an ecosystem of components, services and workflows.

Visuals should reflect this modularity.

Use structures such as:

- cards
- layers
- panels
- nodes
- connections
- flows
- service blocks
- interface modules
- review checkpoints

These structures make abstract systems understandable.

They also help different Lumina visuals feel related to each other.

A viewer should recognise a consistent visual grammar across Lumina Core, Lumina Engine, Lumina Studio and Lumina Experience.

---

## 7. Abstract Interfaces

Interfaces are important in Lumina visuals, but they should usually be abstract.

Use simplified interface elements such as:

- cards
- search fields
- filters
- status panels
- result clusters
- dashboards
- workflow steps
- metadata tags
- charts
- small tables

Avoid realistic product screenshots unless explicitly required.

Abstract interfaces are more timeless and easier to reuse.

They also avoid creating the impression that a visual represents a finished product screen when it is actually explaining a concept.

---

## 8. Academic, Not Generic Corporate

Lumina belongs to the context of ETH Library.

The visual language should feel academic, precise and trustworthy.

It should not look like generic SaaS marketing.

Avoid:

- exaggerated enthusiasm
- stock-photo business scenes
- cartoonish expressions
- overly playful characters
- marketing clichés
- excessive gradients
- generic innovation imagery

The tone should be confident, intelligent and restrained.

---

## 9. Modern, But Not Trend-Driven

Lumina visuals should feel contemporary, but they should not rely on short-lived trends.

Avoid visual styles that may quickly become outdated.

Prefer:

- clean vector-based illustration
- simple geometric forms
- subtle depth
- readable compositions
- strong whitespace
- restrained colour use

The visual identity should still feel appropriate in several years.

---

## 10. Consistent Across Media

The same visual language should work across:

- presentation slides
- web pages
- documentation
- diagrams
- posters
- internal concept papers
- AI-generated illustrations

A visual may be adapted to the medium, but the underlying design language should remain consistent.

Presentation visuals may be simpler and more spacious.

Web visuals may be wider and more atmospheric.

Documentation visuals may be more schematic.

However, all of them should still feel like Lumina.

---

## 11. ETH-Aligned, Lumina-Specific

Lumina visuals should respect the ETH Zurich Corporate Design colour system.

However, Lumina should also have its own recognisable identity within that system.

The Lumina-specific identity is built through:

- ETH Blue as the dominant colour
- strong use of white and black
- restrained secondary use of ETH Petrol and ETH Grey
- calm compositions
- semantic networks
- abstract interfaces
- human-centred knowledge work

Lumina should feel clearly connected to ETH, but not reduced to generic ETH branding.

---

## 12. Explain Through Relationships

Many Lumina topics are relational:

- documents relate to subjects
- metadata relates to collections
- search results relate to meaning
- agents relate to workflows
- APIs relate to services
- users relate to knowledge systems

Use relationships as a central visual principle.

Relationships can be shown through:

- clean connecting lines
- grouped cards
- node clusters
- layered interfaces
- directional flows
- semantic proximity
- highlighted pathways

Connections should be readable and purposeful.

Avoid chaotic networks.

---

## Quality Checklist

A Lumina visual is successful when:

- the main idea is clear
- knowledge remains central
- people and expertise are respected
- technology supports the story
- colour is used with restraint
- the composition feels calm
- the visual hierarchy is readable
- the style feels academic and modern
- the result fits the Lumina ecosystem

If a visual feels loud, generic, futuristic or decorative, it should be simplified.
