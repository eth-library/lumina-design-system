# Design Philosophy

The Lumina Design System defines the visual identity of ETH Lumina.

It is based on a simple principle:

**Lumina makes knowledge more accessible, understandable and connected through human-centred AI.**

The visual language should reflect the values of ETH Library and academic knowledge infrastructures rather than the aesthetics of generic technology marketing.

Lumina is not about machines replacing people.

Lumina is about people, knowledge and intelligent library services working together.

---

## Core Idea

ETH Lumina should be visualised as a calm, trustworthy and intelligent ecosystem for academic libraries.

The design language should communicate:

- trust
- clarity
- precision
- openness
- collaboration
- semantic understanding
- academic quality
- innovation through simplicity

Technology should support the story, not dominate it.

People, knowledge and library services remain the protagonists.

---

## Human-Centred AI

Artificial Intelligence in Lumina should be represented as an enabling layer.

It helps people discover, structure, enrich, connect and reuse knowledge.

AI should appear as:

- assistance
- context
- recommendation
- enrichment
- connection
- workflow support
- semantic understanding

AI should not be represented as an autonomous character, robot, brain or magical force.

The visual language should avoid the impression that technology acts independently from human expertise.

---

## Library Perspective

Lumina belongs to the context of an academic library.

The library should be understood as an active knowledge infrastructure, not only as a place where books are stored.

Suitable visual ideas include:

- collections
- documents
- metadata
- research outputs
- knowledge graphs
- semantic relationships
- discovery journeys
- human expertise
- reusable services
- digital library workflows

The design should make library work visible, modern and valuable.

---

## Technology Perspective

Lumina includes advanced technologies such as embeddings, semantic search, AI agents, APIs, metadata pipelines and knowledge graphs.

These technologies should be visualised in a clear and understandable way.

Prefer visual metaphors such as:

- connected document cards
- structured data flows
- semantic clusters
- abstract interfaces
- knowledge networks
- workflow steps
- review checkpoints
- reusable service layers

Avoid visual metaphors that suggest complexity for its own sake.

The goal is not to show how complicated the technology is.

The goal is to show what the technology enables.

---

## Visual Tone

Lumina visuals should feel:

- calm
- precise
- modern
- academic
- professional
- optimistic
- accessible
- trustworthy

They should not feel:

- loud
- futuristic
- playful
- magical
- corporate-generic
- science-fictional
- visually overloaded

The visual tone should be confident without being promotional.

---

## Design Consequences

Every Lumina visual should have one clear message.

Every visual should help the viewer understand a concept, service or relationship.

Use simplicity as a sign of maturity.

Use whitespace as an active design element.

Use colour with restraint.

Use people to show purpose and expertise.

Use abstract interfaces to explain systems without relying on real screenshots.

Use networks, cards and flows to make knowledge structures visible.

---

## Design Compass

When creating a Lumina visual, use this question as a guide:

**Does this visual make knowledge, people and intelligent library services easier to understand?**

If the answer is yes, the visual is likely aligned with the Lumina Design System.
