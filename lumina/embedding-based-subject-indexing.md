# Embedding-Based Subject Indexing

Embedding-Based Subject Indexing is a strategic functional area within the Lumina ecosystem.

It supports automated and machine-assisted subject indexing by matching bibliographic resources against an embedded controlled vocabulary.

It should be visualised as a semantic matching and enrichment workflow, not as a conventional classification model.

The focus is on making controlled vocabularies computationally useful while keeping subject knowledge transparent, structured and reviewable.

---

## Purpose

Embedding-Based Subject Indexing helps transform bibliographic input into structured subject suggestions.

It may support:

- automated subject indexing
- machine-assisted subject indexing
- semantic matching against controlled vocabularies
- candidate retrieval and ranking
- subject vocabulary enrichment
- metadata enrichment
- semantic normalization
- explainable candidate evidence
- human-in-the-loop review
- quality evaluation
- reusable subject layers for discovery and APIs

The functional area should communicate that subject indexing is not only an automation task.

It is a semantic infrastructure service that improves the quality, consistency and reusability of library data.

---

## Core Message

Embedding-Based Subject Indexing compares documents with an embedded subject vocabulary and returns ranked, explainable subject candidates.

The key message is:

**Embedding-Based Subject Indexing turns controlled vocabularies into searchable semantic spaces for transparent, reviewable and reusable subject enrichment.**

It should communicate:

- semantic matching
- controlled vocabulary grounding
- explainable suggestions
- structured candidate evidence
- human review readiness
- metadata enrichment
- semantic normalization
- reusable subject infrastructure
- responsible automation

It should not feel like a black-box classifier.

It should feel like a transparent matching workflow that supports expert judgement.

## Role in the Lumina Ecosystem

Embedding-Based Subject Indexing belongs primarily to the enrichment and context area of Lumina.

It connects especially strongly to:

- Lumina Engine
- Lumina Core
- Lumina Studio
- Lumina Experience

### Lumina Engine

Lumina Engine performs the semantic processing, embedding search, candidate retrieval, deduplication, ranking and reranking.

Visual relationship:

Engine appears as the active processing layer where bibliographic input is transformed into ranked subject suggestions.

### Lumina Core

Lumina Core provides the shared data, metadata, vocabulary and API foundation.

Visual relationship:

Core appears as the trusted foundation that stores, exposes and reuses the enriched subject layer.

### Lumina Studio

Lumina Studio supports review, monitoring, quality assessment and workflow governance.

Visual relationship:

Studio appears as the place where cataloguers or subject specialists inspect, validate and improve candidate suggestions.

### Lumina Experience

Lumina Experience can use enriched subject metadata for discovery, recommendations, browsing and contextual exploration.

Visual relationship:

Experience appears as a user-facing discovery layer that benefits from better semantic subject enrichment.

## Visual Story

The visual story should be about matching documents to controlled subject concepts through semantic proximity.

Possible story:

A bibliographic record enters the workflow. Its title, abstract, table of contents or other available text are prepared and converted into a semantic representation. The system searches an embedded controlled vocabulary, retrieves candidate subject concepts, deduplicates and ranks them, and presents structured suggestions with evidence for human review.

The visual should show:

- bibliographic input
- text preparation
- embedding or semantic representation
- controlled vocabulary as semantic space
- document-to-vocabulary matching
- ranked subject candidates
- candidate evidence
- human review checkpoint
- confirmed enriched metadata output

The viewer should understand that the vocabulary itself becomes an active semantic retrieval space.

- ETH Blue for the main workflow, central matching elements and confirmed subject structures

Accent colours may be used sparingly:

## Workflow Variants

When visualising the current MVP, the five workflow variants may be represented as comparable paths.

Possible variant labels:

- direct embedding search
- pre-processing plus embedding search
- LLM-derived keywords with joint search
- LLM-derived keywords with separate searches
- pre-processing plus retrieval and reranking

These variants should be shown as evaluation paths, not as final product features.

The emphasis should be on methodological comparison, retrieval quality, candidate diversity, ranking quality and operational suitability.

## Avoid

Do not show Embedding-Based Subject Indexing as:

- a black-box AI classifier
- a neural network diagram
- a robot assigning subjects
- a magical automation process
- a simple keyword search
- a flat spreadsheet of metadata
- a generic recommendation engine
- a purely mathematical vector diagram
- a chaotic cloud of points
- a fully autonomous replacement for cataloguers

Avoid implying that the system produces final truth automatically.

It produces structured, ranked and reviewable subject candidates.
