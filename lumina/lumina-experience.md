# Lumina Experience

Lumina Experience is the user-facing interaction and discovery layer of the Lumina ecosystem.

It makes Lumina services accessible to people through search, exploration, recommendations, assistants, interfaces and guided knowledge workflows.

Lumina Experience should be visualised as clear, helpful and human-centred.

It is the part of Lumina where users experience the value of the underlying Core, Engine and Studio components.

---

## Purpose

Lumina Experience helps people discover, understand and work with academic knowledge.

It may support:

- semantic search
- exploratory discovery
- personalised research support
- recommendation of related resources
- interaction with library collections
- research assistance
- contextual guidance
- knowledge graph exploration
- document discovery
- subject-based navigation
- AI-assisted question answering
- access to enriched metadata
- interaction with digital library services

Lumina Experience should make advanced library services feel accessible and useful.

It should communicate that Lumina helps users find meaning, context and connections.

---

## Core Message

Lumina Experience turns intelligent library services into useful human experiences.

The key message is:

**Lumina Experience helps people discover, explore and understand academic knowledge through clear, intelligent and trustworthy interfaces.**

It should communicate:

- discovery
- access
- clarity
- guidance
- relevance
- trust
- semantic understanding
- human-centred interaction
- intelligent support
- academic usefulness

Lumina Experience should not feel like a generic chatbot interface or a commercial search engine.

It should feel like a thoughtful academic discovery environment.

---

## Role in the Lumina Ecosystem

Lumina Experience is the visible layer of the Lumina ecosystem.

It uses services and outputs from:

- Lumina Core
- Lumina Engine
- Lumina Studio
- semantic search services
- enriched metadata
- knowledge graphs
- APIs
- recommendation services
- subject indexing workflows
- AI-assisted knowledge services

Lumina Experience makes these capabilities accessible through user-facing interfaces.

It should be visualised as the place where people interact with knowledge, not where technical processing happens.

The technical complexity should remain in the background.

The user benefit should be foregrounded.

## Visual Story

The visual story of Lumina Experience should be about guided discovery.

Possible story:

A researcher, student or library user interacts with a calm, intelligent discovery interface. Search results are not shown as a flat list only. Instead, related documents, subject areas, metadata, recommendations and knowledge graph connections help the user understand the context of what they have found.

The visual should show:

- a person exploring knowledge
- a clear search or discovery interface
- connected document cards
- semantic result clusters
- recommendations
- related subjects
- knowledge graph fragments
- contextual guidance
- calm interface panels
- meaningful relationships between resources

The viewer should understand that Lumina Experience helps users move from searching to understanding.

## Preferred Composition

Lumina Experience works especially well in user-centred and discovery-oriented compositions.

Preferred layouts:

### Discovery Interface

A user interacts with a search or discovery interface that shows result cards, filters, semantic clusters and related resources.

Good for explaining semantic search and exploratory discovery.

### Research Workspace

A calm workspace shows documents, notes, recommendations and knowledge connections around a user.

Good for research assistant and knowledge work scenarios.

### Knowledge Map

A visual knowledge network shows how documents, subjects and concepts are connected.

Good for explaining semantic exploration and connected discovery.

### Search Journey

A simple left-to-right or centre-out composition shows a user moving from a question to relevant resources, context and understanding.

Good for presentations to management or non-technical audiences.

### Assistant-Supported Interaction

A subtle assistant panel helps a user explore resources, refine a query or understand results.

Good for AI-supported user services, as long as the assistant does not dominate the visual.

## Colour Emphasis

Use:

- ETH Blue for the main interface, search elements and key discovery structures
- White for clean backgrounds, cards and user-facing clarity
- Black for labels, icons and structural contrast
- ETH Petrol for semantic relationships, recommendations and knowledge graph links
- ETH Grey for secondary interface elements, filters and supporting panels

Accent colours may be used sparingly:

- ETH Green for successful discovery, saved results or validated recommendations
- ETH Purple for AI-assisted suggestions or advanced semantic features
- ETH Bronze for subject categories or thematic groupings
- ETH Red only for warnings or error states

Lumina Experience visuals should feel inviting, but not colourful.

They should feel clear, trustworthy and academically grounded.

## Relationship to Other Components

### Lumina Core

Lumina Core provides the trusted data, metadata, APIs and service foundation.

Lumina Experience uses Core to access reliable knowledge services.

Visual relationship:

Core may appear as a subtle service layer behind or beneath the user-facing interface.

---

### Lumina Engine

Lumina Engine creates semantic signals, enriched metadata, recommendations, embeddings and matching results.

Lumina Experience uses these outputs to provide better discovery and interaction.

Visual relationship:

Engine may appear as a subtle background processing layer that supports result clusters, recommendations or semantic connections.

---

### Lumina Studio

Lumina Studio supports the quality, monitoring and configuration of services that affect Lumina Experience.

Visual relationship:

Studio may appear as an operational layer ensuring that user-facing services are reviewed, maintained and improved.

In user-facing visuals, Studio should usually remain in the background.

## Specific Topic: Semantic Search

For semantic search, show discovery beyond exact keywords.

Suitable visual elements:

- search field
- result clusters
- connected document cards
- semantic relationship lines
- related concepts
- subject filters
- contextual suggestions
- highlighted relevant resources

The visual should communicate that Lumina helps users find resources through meaning, context and relationships.

Avoid showing semantic search as a simple magnifying glass.

Avoid showing endless result lists.

Avoid making the interface feel like a generic web search engine.

---

## Specific Topic: Research Assistant

For a research assistant, show support rather than automation.

Suitable visual elements:

- user workspace
- assistant card or guidance panel
- recommended documents
- related concepts
- summaries or abstract text blocks
- saved resources
- research path
- contextual suggestions
- human decision points

The visual should communicate that the assistant helps the user think, explore and organise knowledge.

Avoid showing the assistant as a robot, avatar or autonomous expert.

Avoid implying that the assistant replaces research judgement.

---

## Specific Topic: Knowledge Exploration

For knowledge exploration, show connected resources in a readable structure.

Suitable visual elements:

- central topic or research question
- related document cards
- subject nodes
- collection clusters
- semantic links
- suggested pathways
- related authors, concepts or organisations
- zoomed-in detail cards

The visual should communicate exploration and orientation.

Avoid chaotic graph networks.

Avoid showing too many nodes or unreadable labels.

---

## Specific Topic: Recommendations

For recommendations, show relevant suggestions as contextual support.

Suitable visual elements:

- recommended resource cards
- "related to this" structures
- grouped suggestions
- confidence or relevance indicators
- semantic connections
- user selection or review

The visual should communicate that recommendations are explainable and connected to context.

Avoid making recommendations look like commercial advertising or shopping suggestions.

---

## Specific Topic: Subject-Based Navigation

For subject-based navigation, show subjects as meaningful pathways into collections.

Suitable visual elements:

- subject labels
- thematic clusters
- document groups
- classification paths
- related topics
- filters
- breadcrumb-like exploration paths

The visual should communicate that users can navigate knowledge through structured subject context.

Avoid showing subject indexing as a flat list of labels only.

---

## Avoid

Do not show Lumina Experience as:

- a generic chatbot
- a commercial search engine clone
- a social media interface
- a shopping recommendation system
- a flashy AI assistant
- a robot conversation
- a cluttered portal
- a dense dashboard
- a dark futuristic interface
- a magical answer machine
- a real product screenshot unless explicitly requested

Avoid implying that Lumina simply gives answers.

Lumina Experience should support discovery, understanding and informed exploration.

---

A strong Lumina Experience visual should show a person engaging with knowledge through a clear and intelligent interface.

It should connect:

- search
- documents

- recommendations

- research workflows

- library services

The image should communicate that Lumina Experience helps people move from information access to meaningful understanding.
