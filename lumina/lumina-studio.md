# Lumina Studio

Lumina Studio is the operational and administrative workspace of the Lumina ecosystem.

It provides visibility, control and review capabilities for Lumina workflows, services and AI-supported processes.

Lumina Studio should be visualised as calm, structured and trustworthy.

It is not a generic dashboard.

It is the place where people understand, monitor, configure and improve intelligent library services.

---

## Purpose

Lumina Studio helps library experts, service owners and technical teams manage Lumina processes.

It may support:

- workflow monitoring
- metadata review
- quality assurance
- configuration of services
- AI-assisted process supervision
- validation of enrichment results
- orchestration of processing jobs
- review of subject indexing suggestions
- service health monitoring
- inspection of API-based processes
- analysis of semantic enrichment outputs
- operational transparency

Lumina Studio should make intelligent processes understandable and controllable.

It should communicate that Lumina is not a black box.

---

## Core Message

Lumina Studio provides transparency and control for intelligent library workflows.

The key message is:

**Lumina Studio allows experts to monitor, review and steer Lumina services with confidence.**

It should communicate:

- overview
- control
- transparency
- quality
- trust
- human judgement
- responsible AI
- operational clarity
- continuous improvement

Lumina Studio should not feel like a surveillance dashboard or a technical control room.

It should feel like a professional knowledge-work environment.

---

## Role in the Lumina Ecosystem

Lumina Studio sits between people and Lumina processes.

It gives human experts access to what Lumina Core and Lumina Engine are doing.

It may interact with:

- Lumina Core
- Lumina Engine
- Lumina Experience
- metadata workflows
- indexing workflows
- enrichment pipelines
- AI agents
- semantic services
- APIs
- review and validation processes
- quality monitoring

Lumina Studio should be visualised as the human-facing operational layer for managing Lumina.

It makes the system observable and trustworthy.

---

## Visual Story

The visual story of Lumina Studio should be about oversight, review and responsible control.

Possible story:

A library expert or service owner works in a calm dashboard environment. The interface shows active workflows, metadata quality indicators, enrichment results, review queues and service status cards. The expert can inspect suggestions, validate outputs and monitor how Lumina processes are performing.

The visual should show:

- dashboard panels
- workflow status cards
- review queues
- metadata quality indicators
- AI-assisted suggestions
- human validation points
- service modules
- process timelines
- alerts or warnings only when necessary
- clear connections to Core and Engine

The viewer should understand that Lumina Studio gives people confidence in the system.

It does not hide complexity.

It organises complexity.

## Preferred Composition

Lumina Studio works especially well in dashboard-oriented and human-in-the-loop compositions.

Preferred layouts:

### Dashboard Workspace

A large central interface shows the current state of Lumina processes.

A person may appear beside it, reviewing or configuring workflows.

Good for explaining Studio as the operational workspace.

### Review Queue

Metadata or subject indexing suggestions appear as structured cards waiting for validation.

A human expert reviews, accepts, edits or rejects suggestions.

Good for responsible AI and human-in-the-loop narratives.

### Workflow Monitoring

Several workflows appear as horizontal or vertical progress tracks.

Status cards show whether processes are running, completed or require review.

Good for operational presentations.

### Quality Overview

Charts, indicators and review panels show the quality of enrichment, matching or indexing results.

Good for management, governance and service quality discussions.

### Orchestration View

Lumina Studio appears as an interface that connects to Engine processes and Core services.

Good for architecture and operational control explanations.

## Colour Emphasis

Use:

- ETH Blue for the main Studio interface, key panels and important controls
- White for dashboard backgrounds and workspace clarity
- Black for labels, icons and structural contrast
- ETH Grey for secondary cards, inactive panels and neutral status elements
- ETH Petrol for service connections, workflow lines and technical relationships

Accent colours may be used sparingly:

- ETH Green for successful workflows, validated results or healthy service states
- ETH Purple for AI-assisted suggestions or advanced semantic features
- ETH Bronze for selected categories or review groups
- ETH Red only for critical errors, warnings or failed states

Lumina Studio visuals may include more small status elements than other Lumina visuals, but the overall colour impression should remain calm.

Avoid dashboards that look colourful, noisy or alarm-heavy.

## Relationship to Other Components

### Lumina Core

Lumina Core provides the shared data, service and API foundation.

Lumina Studio uses Core to access configurations, services, metadata and operational states.

Visual relationship:

Core may appear as a stable service layer underneath or beside the Studio dashboard.

---

### Lumina Engine

Lumina Engine performs processing, enrichment, matching and classification.

Lumina Studio monitors and reviews these processes.

Visual relationship:

Engine may appear as active processing modules connected to Studio through status lines, review queues or workflow panels.

---

### Lumina Experience

Lumina Experience provides user-facing discovery and interaction.

Lumina Studio can support the quality, configuration and monitoring of services that affect Experience.

Visual relationship:

Experience may appear as an output channel or user-facing layer whose quality is supported by Studio monitoring and review.

## Specific Topic: Human-in-the-Loop Review

For human-in-the-loop review, show the human expert as an active reviewer, not as a passive observer.

Suitable visual elements:

- suggestion cards
- confidence indicators
- metadata fields
- subject labels
- accept/edit/reject states
- review queue
- quality summary
- audit trail or process history
- connection to Engine outputs

The visual should communicate that AI can suggest, but people can review, correct and improve.

Avoid showing AI as fully autonomous or always correct.

---

## Specific Topic: Workflow Monitoring

For workflow monitoring, show structured process visibility.

Suitable visual elements:

- active workflows
- process timelines
- status cards
- completion indicators
- queued tasks
- review-needed states
- service health panels
- calm progress visualisation

The visual should communicate operational confidence.

Avoid alarm-heavy control-room visuals.

---

## Specific Topic: Quality Assurance

For quality assurance, show evaluation and improvement.

Suitable visual elements:

- quality indicators
- validation checkpoints
- comparison cards
- before-and-after metadata structures
- review decisions
- improvement trends
- completed validation states

The visual should communicate that Lumina services can be monitored, evaluated and improved over time.

Avoid presenting quality as purely automatic or purely numeric.

---

## Avoid

Do not show Lumina Studio as:

- a dark control room
- a surveillance dashboard
- a cybersecurity command centre
- a financial trading screen
- a cluttered admin interface
- a dense technical monitoring wall
- a generic SaaS analytics dashboard
- a real product screenshot
- a magical AI control panel
- a dashboard full of red alerts

Avoid implying that Studio is only for technical administrators.

It should also feel relevant to library experts and service owners.
